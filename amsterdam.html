Thursday 3/19

The line was 200+ people long at the Anne Frank house, so we went to Pancake Bakery for a delicious breakfast.

P 3.19 09.46 Pancake Bakery P 3.19 09.52 Pancake Bakery P 3.19 09.57 Pancake Bakery P 3.19 10.23 Pancake Bakery upstairs P 3.19 10.24 Pancake Bakery upstairs P 3.19 10.30 Canal

We followed that with a wonderful bike tour with Mike’s Bikes, which included a stop at the Rembrandt Hoeve for a clog making and cheese making demonstration, the Amsterdam forest, and the Vondelpark.

A fantastic tour, but sadly it ended with our worst moment of the trip: the tires had been let out of our rental bikes while were on the tour. Not a huge deal: the nice Mike’s Bikes folks helped us get them reinflated, but in the rush to get moving when that was all done, I left Sam’s backpack hanging on a fence. We returned within 5 minutes, but that was all it took: gone. Phone chargers. Wallet. And worst of all, the cheese!

We returned to the hotel to more scary news: due to the Lufthansa strike in Germany, both of our flights were canceled. After about an hour on the phone at the (very patient) front desk of Inntel, Expedia rebooked us on a direct flight home with Delta. Big exhale (and a thumbs-up to Expedia customer service), as Sam was due to fly out for Grand Cayman early Sunday morning. We had a nice nighttime walk to Burgerlijk Burger for dinner, and then stopped for a last tiramisu at Il Pacioccone.

I have to stop and praise Sam here: his attitude was 100% positive throughout, a rare trait in anyone much less a 13-year-old. He never once flagged in the face of strange food, a stomach bug, miles of walking, hours of museum time, and a stolen backpack. I couldn’t ask for a better travel companion.
