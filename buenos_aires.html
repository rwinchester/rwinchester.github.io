Buenos Aires 2017 - Sam and Rob

Thursday 3/9

We had a good flight out, but it's a long journey: leaving Boston at 2:45 PM, two hours to Atlanta, a few hours' layover there, and then ten hours to Buenos Aires with a 9:10 AM arrival. Luckily, a modern plane means plenty of food, and movies and poker on the seatback video.

Friday 3/10

Our first day. We cabbed to our hotel, the CH Madero Urbano Suites at Azopardo 770, stored our luggage, and walked around exploring the river nearby and Avenue Independencia. We found El Banco Rojo for delicious lunch burgers. Went back the hotel to check in and rest, and then to Gran Parrilla Del Plata for a typical 8:00 PM Buenos Aires dinner. It was an Italian restaurant, but like any good Argentinian restaurant, it featured steak. We washed the steaks down with a nice Malbec while a giant German birthday party dominated the background.

Saturday 3/11

We started the day with a leisurely late-morning Starbucks by the water. In the afternoon, we took our now-traditional bike tour of the city, courtesy of B.A. Bikes. We toured the north side of the city, mostly through Retiro, Ricoleta, and Palermo, including a stop at the famous Recoleta Cemetery. That took most of the afternoon, and by the end we were very hungry, so we followed the good advice of B.A. Bikes and stopped for an amazing pizza at Pizzería Güerrín. We followed that with a long walk home down Avenue 9 de Julio, the widest avenue in the world.

Sunday 3/12

Sunday was San Telmo Fair day. We wandered up and down for a couple of hours, including a stop at a pop-up barbecue with live music. After that we walked back down to the river and over to the wealthy side of Puerto Madero, which we dubbed "Dubai" because of all the fancy construction. We stopped riverside at the Lobo Cafe for fancy coffee, and then back to the hotel for some hot tub and pool time. We went back to El Banco Rojo for amazing house tacos for dinner and Freddo for a quick gelato dessert.

Monday 3/13

Museum Day. We Übered to MALBA where we saw a lot of impressive art, including some Frida Kahlos. We followed up with a nice long walk to Recoleta Cemetery to take the pictures we'd missed out on the day before. The nice sunny day called for a cafe lunch, and while the food was not good, the location was perfect. Besides, we were saving up for the highlight of the day: dinner at Don Julio. We kept dinner simple in the Argentinian style: incredible steaks and a nice Malbec. We Übered back to the Obelisco de Buenos Aires after dinner for another photo op, and then headed back down the Avenue 9 de Julio toward home. The long walk wore us out, so we were forced to stop into Freddo for another gelato to cap off the day.

Tuesday 3/14

Our last day, but with a late flight, we still had a lot of the day to explore. We had our final in-room breakfast (a daily feature of the hotel), walked across the river for more fancy coffee at Lobo Cafe, and chilled by pool and worked out for a while. A final walk to the river for a last Starbucks in the sun and it was time to Über to airport. Our 8:15 PM flight was due into Atlanta at 8:45 AM the next day. We were lucky to have just missed a big snowstorm; our flights were on time and we landed back in Boston on time around 11:30 AM.
